My Courses

Set each course's SSA (the score a 1000-rated player shoots). Points/throw defaults to 10 — refine it for accuracy. Changes save automatically.

How do I find a course's SSA?
  1. Go to pdga.com and open an event's results page for the course, layout, and division you play.
  2. Look at the score and round rating columns.
  3. The SSA is the score that lines up with a 1000 round rating. Find a round rated right around 1000 — that player's score is the SSA. (Or pick two players and estimate the score halfway to 1000.)
  4. Type that score into the course's SSA field above.
  5. Optional (more accurate): set Points/throw from two players: (ratingA − ratingB) ÷ (scoreB − scoreA).

Formula used: Rating = 1000 + (SSA − Score) × Points/throw. SSA shifts with layout, weather, and season, so update it now and then.
